Governance decisions are rarely simple

Every governance reform sits somewhere on a spectrum between central control and local autonomy. Sebenza helps decision makers understand where their organisation currently sits, what the alternatives would actually mean in practice, and how to move deliberately rather than by accident.

01

Centralised models

Strong financial control, consistent reporting and lower administrative cost — at the price of local ownership and responsiveness.

02

Polycentric models

Distributed decision making that builds community ownership and legitimacy, but requires real investment in coordination and capability.

03

Hybrid models

Centralised accountability paired with decentralised decision making — increasingly the direction taken by the strongest governance reforms internationally.

No governance model is universally superior. The right structure depends on risk appetite, administrative capacity and how much local autonomy is genuinely a strategic objective.

Accountability and self-determination are not opposites. The strongest governance reforms internationally have found ways to strengthen both at once.

Structure only gets you so far. Governance effectiveness ultimately depends on the capability of the organisations working within it.
