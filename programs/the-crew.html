The Crew

IRAAC's broad community program — skills, connection and participation, carried by trusted local people and backed end-to-end by proper funding and reporting.

What The Crew is

The Crew is IRAAC's broader community-facing program, focused on building skills, connection and participation across the community rather than within a single age group or cohort. Where MCC works organisation-to-organisation and YouthScape works with young people, The Crew is the program through which community members more widely take part in — and benefit from — the work IRAAC is building.

It runs through IRAAC and is supported end-to-end by the funding, reporting and administrative frameworks Sebenza maintains: proposals written to a standard funders trust, delivery tracked consistently, and outcomes reported through the same digital office as every other program.

The shape of the program

Like YouthScape, The Crew is still early in its public story, and the specific activities and people it supports are still being fleshed out in IRAAC's own materials — so this page describes the shape of the program honestly rather than dressing it up with invented detail.

The shape itself is the point: a community program whose priorities come from what community members actually said they need, whose delivery is carried by trusted local people, and whose results are captured well enough that continued funding is an evidence-based decision rather than an act of faith. As The Crew's activities are documented, this page will carry the specifics.

How community voice drives it

Every program IRAAC runs starts from the same place: what individuals and families said they need, in their own words, through the people and programs they already trust. The Crew is where that principle applies most broadly — it's the program with the widest front door, open to the whole community rather than a defined cohort.

Sebenza's reporting systems then close the loop: they carry what community said all the way up to Boards, funders and government, so decisions about the program are made with community voice in the room — not lost somewhere between the ground and the top.
