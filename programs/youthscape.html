YouthScape

IRAAC's program for young people in community — connection to culture, opportunity and support, built on a governance foundation strong enough to carry it.

What YouthScape is

YouthScape is IRAAC's program for young people in community, built around connection to culture, opportunity and support. It sits inside the same architecture as everything else IRAAC runs: community voice shapes what the program does, delivery happens through people and relationships the community already trusts, and the governance and reporting foundation underneath means the program's story can be told credibly to Boards, funders and government.

For a youth program in particular, that foundation matters. Funders rightly hold work with young people to a high standard of accountability, and YouthScape is being built to meet that standard from day one rather than retrofitting it later — proper funding and acquittal processes, consistent reporting, and Board oversight from the start.

An honest picture of where it's at

YouthScape is an early-stage program, and this page deliberately doesn't overstate it. Its detailed activities, the young people it works with and its outcomes are still being documented in IRAAC's own materials. As that documentation firms up, this page will grow with it — real activities, real stories told with community consent, and real reported outcomes rather than generic youth-program language.

What can be said now is how it will be run: designed with community rather than for it, carried by trusted local people, and reported through the same centralised systems as IRAAC's other programs — so that what young people and families say they need is what Boards and funders actually see.

Why the foundation matters for young people

Programs for young people live and die on continuity. A program that loses its funding mid-year, or can't demonstrate its impact when a contract comes up for renewal, breaks trust with the very young people it set out to support. The governance and reporting backbone under YouthScape exists to prevent exactly that — making continued funding an evidence-based decision rather than an act of faith.

That's the Sebenza contribution in a sentence: we don't run the program, community does. We make sure the program can prove itself, year after year, so it gets to keep running.
