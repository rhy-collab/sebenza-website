Approach

We give you the evidence and the trade-offs, not a predetermined answer.

Governance reform is ultimately a policy choice. Our job is to make that choice easier by setting out clearly what each pathway would actually mean — for accountability, for cost, for staffing, and for the people the system is meant to serve.

What we do

Sebenza offers four related services, usually beginning with an exploratory conversation to understand which of these is the right starting point.

Governance options analysis

Structured discussion papers comparing centralised, polycentric and hybrid governance models, drawing on international examples and applied to your specific context.

Organisational and structural review

An independent look at existing structures, staffing, reporting systems and governance frameworks to identify duplication, gaps and opportunities for stronger assurance.

Governance capability assessment

A structured maturity assessment for community organisations, covering governance readiness, financial management and reporting capability.

Capability building programs

Practical, peer-led programs that strengthen governance capability directly within organisations — designed to work regardless of which governance pathway is ultimately chosen.

How an engagement typically runs

Every engagement is shaped around the question at hand, but most follow a similar shape.

01

Exploratory conversation

We start by understanding the question you're actually facing, the constraints you're working within, and what decision this work needs to support.

02

Evidence and context

We draw on governance theory, international case studies and comparable reforms to build a grounded picture of what's realistic and what the trade-offs would be.

03

Options, not recommendations

We set out the pathways available, with their governance, staffing, cost and reporting implications made explicit — so the choice stays with you.

04

Practical next steps

Where useful, we identify low-risk, high-value actions that improve outcomes regardless of which broader direction is ultimately taken.

Principles we work by

We don't advocate for a predetermined governance outcome. Our value is in setting out the options clearly, not in pushing a particular structure.

Accountability and participation aren't a trade-off to be minimised — the strongest reforms strengthen both together.

We would rather be asked for more help than offer more than is wanted. Every engagement starts small and grows only if it's useful.
