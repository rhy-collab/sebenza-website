Services

Practical support across strategy, governance, funding and digital capability.

Every engagement starts with a conversation about what your organisation actually needs — not a fixed package. Most of our work falls into one of the four areas below.

01

Strategy & organisational growth

Strategic planning, government engagement and orchestration, and advice as your organisation takes on new entities, assets, staff and larger management structures. Built for organisations that are growing and need a steady hand on the bigger picture.

02

Governance & compliance

Board reporting frameworks, governance training and independent Board evaluation, and written financial and operational procedures — so your governance meets ORIC, ACNC, Aboriginal Affairs NSW, LDM and funder expectations, and you can prove it.

03

Funding & reporting

Program and funding proposal development, and external reporting packs covering quarterly reports, annual reports, acquittals and audits — built once and reused, rather than rebuilt from scratch every time.

04

Digital capability

A simple, reliable digital office — email, shared drives, documents and reporting tools — set up and taught in plain language, with training materials your organisation can reuse for every new starter.

On-site capability building

Alongside these four areas, we offer hands-on, on-site training — working with your staff on real budgets, real reports and real funding applications, so capability stays in your organisation once the engagement ends.

For staff and Board Members

Practical training in financial management, bookkeeping, reporting, governance and funding applications, delivered around your team's real workload.

For growing organisations

Ongoing administration support — remote or on site — for organisations adopting new systems, until your team can run them with confidence on its own.

How an engagement typically runs

We start with a conversation, not a proposal — understanding the real problem before recommending anything.

We build systems and train your people to run them, rather than creating ongoing dependence on outside support.

We'd rather be asked for more help than offer more than is wanted.
