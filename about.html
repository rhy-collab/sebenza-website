About

A boutique practice, built to give straight, independent advice.

Sebenza means "to work" in isiZulu — a name chosen to reflect a practical, hands-on approach to governance advisory, rather than a purely theoretical one.

Sebenza Consulting is led by Simone Coombes, working directly with a small number of clients at a time rather than through layers of delivery staff. That means the person who scopes an engagement is the same person doing the analysis and writing the advice.

Why Sebenza exists

Governance reform is often treated as a technical exercise — restructure the org chart, redraw the reporting lines, and move on. In practice, it's a deeply human question about trust: how much a government trusts communities to make their own decisions, and how much communities trust government to keep its word on funding and support. Sebenza was set up to bring evidence and structure to that conversation, without pretending it's simpler than it is.

Independent by design

We don't run delivery programs that create an incentive to recommend a particular structure. Our only product is clear, evidence-based advice.

Grounded in international practice

Our analysis draws on governance reform across Canada, the United States, New Zealand, Switzerland, Spain and Australia — not just domestic precedent.

Focused on First Nations governance

Particular depth in Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander governance, self-determination, Local Decision Making and community-controlled organisations.

Built around trust, not volume

We would rather be asked for more help than offer more than is wanted — engagements grow because they're useful, not because they were oversold.

How we see our role

We're a decision support partner, not a decision maker. The governance pathway a government or community ultimately chooses is theirs — our job is to make sure that choice is well informed.

Every recommendation we make has to survive being read by someone who disagrees with it. That's the standard we hold our own analysis to.
